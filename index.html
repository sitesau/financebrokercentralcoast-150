Refinancing Central Coast Explained

The mathematics of switching revolves around the gap between your current rate and new offers. For a typical loan balance of $600,000, a reduction of just 0.5 percentage points can save approximately $3,000 in interest each year. However, these savings must be weighed against discharge fees and potential break costs if you are exiting a fixed term early. The break-even point typically falls between six and twelve months for standard switches, making the move financially sound if you plan to stay in the home for longer. When assessing refinancing central properties, local growth in equity since 2020 can also improve your loan-to-value ratio, often securing sharper rates than you initially received.

Unlocking Property Equity

Property values across suburbs like Gosford, Wyong and Terrigal have increased substantially since 2020, giving many homeowners more equity than they realise. This capital growth allows you to access funds for renovations or investment deposits without taking on a separate high-interest personal loan. Lenders price aggressively for borrowers with an LVR below 60 or 70 per cent, meaning your improved equity position could unlock rates that were unavailable when you first purchased. Using the equity built in your home to consolidate debt or fund improvements is a common strategy, provided the total borrowing remains within manageable serviceability limits and you do not overcapitalise.

How a Broker Streamlines the Process

Navigating the refinance market alone involves researching individual policies, eligibility criteria and fee structures for dozens of banks. A finance broker performs this assessment by reviewing your current loan structure and comparing it against a panel of more than 50 lenders. They factor in not just the headline rate, but ongoing fees and repayment features to find the effective rate over time. Under the Best Interests Duty, they are legally required to recommend a loan that suits your specific situation rather than the one that pays them the highest commission. This service is generally free to the borrower, as brokers are paid by the lender upon settlement, with no obligation to proceed.

Evaluating Cashback Incentives

Many lenders advertise cashback offers of $2,000 to $4,000 to entice switchers. While attractive, these incentives can sometimes mask a higher interest rate that ultimately costs more over the life of the loan. To determine if a cashback deal is worthwhile, you must calculate the total interest payable over three years with and without the incentive. In many scenarios, a loan with a lower ongoing rate and no cashback provides greater savings after the first 18 months. A broker can model these comparisons in writing, ensuring you see the long-term financial picture rather than just the immediate cash injection.

  1. Review your current loan. Check your current interest rate, remaining term and exit fees to understand your starting position.
  2. Consult a broker. Speak with an accredited broker who can assess your situation and compare products from over 50 lenders.
  3. Compare effective rates. Look at the total cost including fees, not just the headline rate, to find the best deal for your timeframe.
  4. Settle the switch. Submit your application; the new lender pays out the old one, usually within two to six weeks.

Common questions

How long does the refinance process take? The timeline typically ranges from two to six weeks from the date of application, depending on how quickly your current lender processes the discharge.

Will I need a new property valuation? Yes, lenders usually require a new valuation to confirm the current market value of your property, which determines your loan-to-value ratio and the equity you can access.

Is using a broker expensive? For most home and investment loans, the service is free to you as the borrower. Brokers are paid commission by the lender if and when a loan settles, with full disclosure obligations.
